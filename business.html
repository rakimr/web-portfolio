My International Marketing professor asked me to choose a country I thought an American brand would do well in.
I chose Petsmart as the brand and Australia as the country and to my surprise there wasn't a lot of data to work with.
But with enough research I was able to conjure up decent reasoning as to why Petsmart would succeed in Australia.
For this project, I used google sheets and python as tools to help me get my point across.
I will give a full breakdown of how I used these tools, then afterwards link the research paper.
If you'd like, you can skip straight to the research paper linked at the end of the page.

Petsmart_Products

On the Petsmart_Products sheet, I used kaggle.com to find and download a Petsmart retail product dataset in the form of a csv file.
I then inserted that file onto the sheet, cleaned the dataset, and created the column "Animal Type."
The "Animal Type" column was created using functions =SPLIT(text, delimiter, [split_by_each], [remove_empty_text]), to seperate the contents that make up the "Item Description" values, and =FIND(search_for, text_to_search, [starting_at]) to find the key animal names among the split values.
These formulas arent on display, due to having to copy and paste as values in order to filter them.
The pie chart you see here will be featured in the research report.

Aussie_Ownership

On the Aussie_Ownership sheet, there is pet ownership data being sourced from a national australian survey held by Newgate Research.
This data was extracted using the Get Data from PDF macro on Excel, then pasted into this sheet.
I was then able to create a bar chart, reflecting the data.
This bar chart will be featured in the research report.

Pet_Expenses

On the Pet_Expenses sheet, I am sourcing data from the same PDF file as the Aussie_Ownership sheet is sourcing from.
This data consists of the annual expenses of Australian pet owners by category.
To understand it a bit easier, I've created a bar chart for this data table as well.
The bar chart will be seen in the research report.

Correlation

On the Correlation sheet, there is data that is being sourced from the World Happiness Report.
I downloaded the data right from their website worldhappiness.report. It consists of each countries Happiness score, filled with categorical data that is believed to be what constitutes happiness.
I was able to find the significant and positive correlation between Social Support and Happiness by creating a scatter plot using both of those variables' respective columns.
This chart will be seen in the research report.

Research Report

All of the data I cleaned, analyzed, and visualized in the Petsmart Australia workbook was organized and put together to create the Research Report you see below.
The slides are here for you to have a more visual experience when reading the report.
I feature PetBarn locations, which would theoretically be Petsmart's competitor.
Also in slides 7 and 8, I feature a couple of lines of python code used to create bar charts from a South Australian Dog Owner dataset sourced from kaggle.com
The first line of python code df.groupby("Region")["Animal_Type"].value_counts().sort_values().plot("bar") computes a bar chart for the top regions of Australia with dog owners.
The second line of python code df.groupby("Suburb")["Animal_Type"].value_counts().sort_values().tail().plot("bar") dives a tad deeper computing a bar chart for the top suburbs of Australia with dog owners.
Of course, this can all be found in the report below.
